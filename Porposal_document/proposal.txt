% =================== INTRODUCTION ===================
\section{Introduction}
\vspace*{2cm}

In today's freelancing world, many online platforms such as Upwork have created new ways for freelancers to connect with professional clients. Success on such platforms depends on proposal writing, which is the first impression of freelancer on the client. Many freelancers find it difficult to write professional and attractive proposals, which is time-consuming and requires skill and experience.

The Upwork Proposal Assistant is a software that solves these problems. It automates the process of writing proposals using smart templates based on user history or client requirements and helps freelancers improve their chances of selection. It also acts as a learning tool for freelancers to improve their writing skills over time.

\vfill 
\newpage

% =================== PROBLEM STATEMENT ===================
\section{Problem Statement}

Freelancers often struggle to write professional proposals quickly, reducing their chances of selection. Writing proposals manually is time-consuming and requires skill and experience. Additionally, proposals must address client-specific needs effectively; generic proposals often fail to capture attention. 

The Upwork Proposal Assistant provides an automated, efficient solution, generating professional and personalized proposals quickly while helping users improve their proposal-writing skills.

\section{Stakeholders}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]\itemsep0.5em
    \item \textbf{Freelancer (End User):} Uses the system to create and submit proposals.
    \item \textbf{Administrator:} Manages system functionality, ensures ethical use, and handles bug fixes.
    \item \textbf{Developer:} Ensures smooth functionality, adds/updates features, and resolves technical issues. (Explicitly included as a stakeholder)
    \item \textbf{Project Supervisors:} Bridge between developers and administrative team, ensures quality delivery.
    \item \textbf{Investors/External Platform:} May integrate software to enhance user experience.
\end{itemize}

% =================== METHODOLOGY ===================
\section{Software Development Methodology}

The \textbf{Agile Scrum methodology} will be adopted for this project, supporting incremental progress and continuous iterations. Work will be divided into short sprints, with daily stand-ups, sprint reviews, and backlog refinement. This ensures flexibility, collaboration with stakeholders, and continuous improvement.

\subsection*{Tentative Development Schedule (One Year)}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]\itemsep0.3em
    \item \textbf{Months 1-2: Requirements Gathering \& Planning}  
    \item \textbf{Months 3-5: Sprint 1-3 (Core Features Development)}  
    \item \textbf{Months 6-8: Sprint 4-6 (Advanced Features)}  
    \item \textbf{Months 9-10: Sprint 7-8 (Testing \& Refinement)}  
    \item \textbf{Months 11-12: Deployment \& Maintenance}  
\end{itemize}

Throughout the development process, bi-weekly sprint reviews will be conducted with the Requirement Provider to ensure alignment with project goals and user expectations.
